Interesting Facts About Chameleon.

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the inguana suborder.
  • Changing skin colours is an important part of communication among chameleons.
  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Interesting Facts About Camel.

  • Camels are mammals with long-legs, a big-lipped snout and a humped back.
  • There are two types of camels: dromedary camels, which have one hump, and bactrian camels, which have two humps.
  • Camel's hump consist of stored fat, which they can metabolize when food and water is scarce.

Interesting Facts About Teddy Bear.

  1. A Teddy Bear is a stuffed toy in the form of bear.
  2. Since the creation of the first teddy bears which sought to imitiate thr forms of real bear cubs, "teddies" have greatly varied in form, style, color and material.
  3. Teddy Bears are among the most popular gifts for children and are often given to adults to signify love, affection, congratulations, or sympathy.

Interesting Facts About Panda.

  • Like Domestic cats, giant pandas have vertical slits for pupils.
  • At 5 months after their birth, giant pandas learn how to climb - something practicing by climbing on their mum.
  • Giant Pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo.

Interesting Facts About Flying Cicada.

  1. There are around 3,000 cicada species, according to National Geographic, so they vary in size from 0.75 to 2.25 in (2.2 to 5.5 cm) long.
  2. Cicadas can be black, brown or green and can have red, white or blue eyes.
  3. Their wings are transparent and can seem rainbow-hued when held up to a light source.
